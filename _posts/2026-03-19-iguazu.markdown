Today we left Rio for Foz de Iguaçu in the state of Paraná.

We shared the flight with so many Indians. It seemed like they were on a tour to see Iguazu Falls (obviously). We landed safely around 2 pm and headed to our hotel in the central part of Foz do Iguaçu, the city on the Brazilian side. Three countries meet here: Argentina, Brazil and Paraguay. The three cities on the border have a total population of around 800 000 people with the biggest being Ciudad del Este, Paraguay. Puerto de Iguazú on the Argentinian side is the smallest.

![Aerial]({{ '/assets/images/260319/aerial.jpeg' | relative_url }})

Our hotel, Aguas do Iguazu, was located on a busy restaurant street in the center of the city. We arrived there around 3 pm and checked in. It's a nice hotel with a pool. We just took it easy the first day, went in the pool with a couple of beers and relaxed. Later in the evening we went for dinner at a Lebanese restaurant that had manuy vegan options. It was very yummy and we were completeky stuffed after the meal. For some reason there were a lot of arab restaurants in Foz do Iguaçu. I don't know how many shawarma places we passed...

![Town]({{ '/assets/images/260319/foztown.jpeg' | relative_url }})

![Selfie]({{ '/assets/images/260319/townselfie.jpeg' | relative_url }})

It was mostly a day of travelling a taking a breather before seeing the main attraction - the falls.

For dinner we went to a lebanese restaurant and had very good hummus and falafel etc.
