Bom dia!

We thought we would be one of the first people out by Ipanema beach this morning at 6:30, but there were already so many people jogging, playing beach volleyball and exercising already. It seems like Rio has a very large fitness community.

Ipanema beach at 6:30am
![Ipanema]({{ '/assets/images/260318/ipanema-morning.jpeg' | relative_url }})

After an early morning swim, we took a walk around the Lagoa Rodrigo de Freitas (a lake in the south zone).

We arrived at Jardim Botanico (The Botanical Gardens) at 11am and lined up to get a ticket.

The gardens had a lot of preserved wildlife as well as a cactus garden.

Botnical garden
![Garden]({{ '/assets/images/260318/garden.jpeg' | relative_url }})

After the gardens we bought some quick lunch at the supermarket and had a picnic on Ipanema beach. The beach was full of people, and every 30 seconds there would be a vendor passing by selling all kinds of things from drinks and clothes to food like corn and acai.

Ipanema beach
![Ipanema]({{ '/assets/images/260318/beach.jpeg' | relative_url }})

For dinner, we went to have some Poke bowls right up the street. The owner was extremely friendly and gave us some free 'deep fried tofu' - a new dish he had never made.

Poke bowl
![Poke]({{ '/assets/images/260318/poke.jpeg' | relative_url }})

We asked him if he had any recommendations for a good caipirinha. He said we should check out the Padeira Ipanema (Ipanema Bakery) where they have a rooftop bar. We were very surprised to find that it was a huge bakery where once you go in, you can walk up to the rooftop (while passing the kitchen) and there is a restaurant/bar. We ordered a cashew + holy grass and seriguela caiprinha. Very interesting flavours. Then we had some 'Chopp' and called it a night.

Ipanema Bakery
![IpanemaBakery]({{ '/assets/images/260318/caipi.jpeg' | relative_url }})

Chopp
![Chopp]({{ '/assets/images/260318/chop.jpeg' | relative_url }})

We managed to make it through Rio without getting our phones stolen. Tomorrow we leave for Iguazu Falls.

What to say about Rio after spending three days here? First, the good. Ipanema beach is great. You could spend all day there swimming, eating, drinking, exercising. The number of beach vendors might be a put/off for some, but they were all nice and didn't intrudeif you just say no. Overall a great time. 

The scenery around the city is amazing. Forested mountains all around with steep, rocky sides. And the ocean as well, of course. Going up a mountain to get a view is a must, probably. 

Now, the not so good. Rio is loud. Everywhere we went there was construction making noise. And if that wasn't the case there was the constant sound of the city's hectic traffic. Going by car felt somewhat unsafe with all the motorcyclists zig-zagging between the cars and honking their horns. Im just glad I didn't have to drive myself...

The inequality is palpable in Rio. The favelas are nestled in between gated communities. Even in Ipanema, an affluent neighborhood, there were plenty of desperate people living in the street. 

Anyway, we had a good time, would come back again!
