Today we went on a tour to the Baltinache lagoons. There are 7 lagoons in the area which formed from the ocean drying up many years ago. They all have different hues depending on their depth and contain up to 25% salt. There was a sunscreen ban when entering the area as they are trying to preserve the lagoons which have been altered by human activity. Because of this, only one lagoon is allowed for swimming at the moment. Because of the high salt content, you can hardly swim in the lagoon - just float.

After the lagoon tour we rested up a bit before heading to the stargazing tour at around 21:00. We waited for around  40 minutes before the tour guide decided we could get on the van to go to a place less polluted wtih light. The night was very cloudy, but thankfully it had started to clear up. The atacama night sky was really magical. We were first taken to part of the desert to have professional photos taken (which was apparently included in the tour). Afterwards, we moved to another spot to get a lecture on the different constellations and planets. It was weird and beautiful seeing the night sky from the southern hemisphere.
