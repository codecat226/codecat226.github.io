Today we decided to rent mountain bikes and go to the Garaganta del Diablo (Devil’s throat) ourselves instead of going with an organised tour. We got up bright and early so we could start cycling before it got really hot. 

![Biking]({{ '/assets/images/260326/biking.jpeg' | relative_url }})

Cycling through through the canyons was so much fun! They were like nothing we’ve seen before, as if taken from some sci-if movie. Twisting and turning through the landscape they guided us over small hills and through tiny caves. At a few points we had to get off our bikes and carry them up a ledge. Finally we got out of the maze and into view came a glorious little mountain. Let’s climb!

At the top of the mountain we could see over the whole valley as well as the volcano in the distance. We ate our packed sandwiches while admiring the view.

![Canyon]({{ '/assets/images/260326/canyon.jpeg' | relative_url }})

On the way back we rode 2 more kilometres to a small church which was quite underwhelming. Kaj had fun splashing through the water while cycling there. 

In the evening we visited the Meteorite museum in San Pedro. It was a small museum but very interactive and we were able to touch the meteorites and learn the differences between normal rocks and space rocks from the staff who worked there.

![Meteorite]({{ '/assets/images/260326/meteorite.jpeg' | relative_url }})
