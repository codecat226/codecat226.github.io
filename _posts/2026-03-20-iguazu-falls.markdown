The second day we got up early and had breakfast at the hotel. Brazilian breakfast always has plenty of fresh fruit which is very nice :) 

After breakfast we got an uber to the Parque Nacional do Iguzu, which has views of the waterfalls from the brazilian side. Before that though we decided to pay a visit to Parque do Aves (park of birds) which is a bird sanctuary located right next to the entrance to the national park. 

In the bird park they keep and breed many endangered species of birds found in the atlantic rainforest (which is different from Amazonas). Deforestation adn climate change has resulted in the extinction of many species and even more are at risk.

![Bird]({{ '/assets/images/260320/bird.jpeg' | relative_url }})

To avoid the overpriced tourist food inside the national park, we brought our own lunch of flatbreads, hummus, fruit and nuts. After a quick lunch in the shade, we finally headed to Iguazu national park.

The tickets for the national park were based on a sepcific entrance time, so there was hardly any queue when we got there. We waited for an open deck shuttle bus to take us to the start of the trail to the falls.

The trail to the first look of the falls took around 10 minutes. It is hard to describe how it felt to see the falls for the first time. It looked like something out of this world.

![FirstLook]({{ '/assets/images/260320/firstlook.jpeg' | relative_url }})

We also ran into some wild coatis on the trail. The name 'coati' comes from the Tupi native language of Brazil. It means "belt-nose" and is in reference to the coati's sleeping positing - curled up with its snout tucked in its belly.

![Coatis]({{ '/assets/images/260320/coati.jpeg' | relative_url }})

The best part was that as we continued through the trail, we realized that the falls that we had seen was just a tiny part of the waterfall system.

Under the main fall it was almost raining on us.

![Waterfall]({{ '/assets/images/260320/waterfall2.jpeg' | relative_url }})

At the end of the trail, we got onto the shuttle bus that would take us back to the entrance. While on the bus we heard an announcement that the next stop would have a 2.8km hiking trail called San Jose. We made an impromptu decision to get off at the station and do the trail as we had some time to spare before the park closed for the day.

![Jaguar]({{ '/assets/images/260320/jaguar.jpeg' | relative_url }})

The hike turned out to be a pretty narrow trail with no other people in sight. At the start there was a sign on how to react when coming across a jaguar. It definitely set the mood for the hike :D

The hike took 1 hour and it had so many beautiful viewing points also with smaller waterfalls. The only thing we missed out on was being able to swim in the nature as the water level was too high and there were signs to not enter.

![Hike]({{ '/assets/images/260320/hike.jpeg' | relative_url }})

After a very dangerous uber ride back to the town, we took a dip in the pool and then headed out for dinner.

For some reason, the town of Foz de Iguazu has a lot of Shawarma restaurants. We decided to try one of them and ordered a veggie meat patty plate with bean sauce, fries and salad. It was very very tasty.

![Food]({{ '/assets/images/260320/food.jpeg' | relative_url }})
